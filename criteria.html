Criteria

Choosing the right location to live and work is about more than just geography. It’s about finding a place that supports personal and professional growth – where the infrastructure, culture, and opportunities align with your values and goals.

On this page, I’ve outlined the key criteria I use when evaluating potential work destinations. These include career opportunities in tech, access to learning, language and cultural fit, community, quality of life, and long-term sustainability. Each factor plays a role in shaping a balanced and future-ready work environment.

NextDesk criteria

  • Tech / Job Market

    How strong is the local tech industry? A dynamic startup ecosystem, access to established companies, and a solid demand for digital professionals are key factors for building a sustainable career abroad.

  • Learning and Development Opportunities

    Continued learning is essential. Locations with good access to training programs, tech meetups, or academic institutions offer better long-term growth – both professionally and personally.

  • Language and Cultural Access

    Understanding the local language and culture supports integration and makes daily life easier. At the same time, a certain level of accessibility through widely spoken languages like English can be critical for entering the job market.

  • International Community

    An open, international environment helps with networking and cultural exchange. Especially in globally oriented industries, a diverse community can improve both well-being and professional opportunities.

  • Housing and Quality of Life

    Factors like housing availability, safety, infrastructure, and access to leisure activities significantly influence overall quality of life. A good location should support both work and everyday living.

  • Sustainability & Future Orientation

    Long-term perspectives matter. Countries or cities with a strong focus on environmental responsibility, digital infrastructure, and forward-thinking policies are more likely to offer stability and resilience for the future.
