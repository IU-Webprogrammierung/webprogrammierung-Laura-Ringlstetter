Barcelona

Barcelona combines a vibrant cultural scene, a pleasant Mediterranean climate, and a growing job market—especially in tech, design, and tourism. With a good work-life balance, strong infrastructure, and international environment, it’s an attractive city for professionals seeking both career and quality of life.

Casa Milà, commonly known as La Pedrera, is one of Antoni Gaudí’s architectural masterpieces. Built between 1906 and 1912, it’s known for its undulating stone façade and wrought-iron balconies. The rooftop, with its surreal chimney sculptures, is a standout feature and a prime example of Gaudí’s unique style. The building is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and remains a symbol of modernist innovation.

The Sagrada Família is Gaudí’s most iconic and ambitious project. Construction began in 1882 and continues to this day. Its intricate facades and soaring towers reflect deep religious symbolism and exceptional craftsmanship. As one of the most visited monuments in Europe, the basilica represents the spiritual and artistic heart of Barcelona.
