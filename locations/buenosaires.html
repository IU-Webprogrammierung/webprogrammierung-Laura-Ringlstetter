Buenos Aires

Buenos Aires offers a dynamic mix of European influence and Latin American energy. The city is a regional hub for finance, tech, and the creative industries, with a growing startup scene. Affordable living costs, a rich cultural life, and a lively social atmosphere make it an appealing destination for professionals and digital nomads.

The Obelisk is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city. Erected in 1936 to commemorate the founding of Buenos Aires, it stands 67 meters tall at the intersection of Avenida 9 de Julio—one of the widest avenues in the world. The monument is a central meeting point and a symbol of national pride, often lit up for public celebrations and events.

The Puente de la Mujer (Bridge of the Woman) is a striking modern pedestrian bridge located in the Puerto Madero district. Designed by Spanish architect Santiago Calatrava, its asymmetrical, rotating structure symbolizes a couple dancing tango. The bridge reflects the city’s blend of tradition and modernity and is a key feature of Buenos Aires’ revitalized waterfront.
