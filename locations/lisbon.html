Lisbon

Lisbon is one of Europe’s rising tech hubs, offering a mix of innovation, affordability, and quality of life. With a mild climate, growing startup ecosystem, and strong international community, the city attracts professionals looking for career opportunities in a relaxed and inspiring setting.

The historic Tram 28 is a symbol of Lisbon’s charm. It winds through narrow streets, steep hills, and historic neighborhoods like Alfama and Graça, offering both practical transport and a scenic tour of the city’s past. The vintage yellow trams have become an icon of Lisbon’s identity and daily life.

The Palácio Nacional de Sintra, also known as the National Palace of Sintra, is easily recognized by its two large conical chimneys rising from the kitchen towers. Located just outside Lisbon in the town of Sintra, the white palace dates back to the Middle Ages and showcases a blend of Gothic, Moorish, and Manueline styles. It served as a royal residence for centuries and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, reflecting Portugal’s rich architectural and cultural history.
